The racial breakdown of UC applicants and admitted students has changed dramatically since 1990, but it's largely kept pace with demographic shifts in California.

In 1990, when white people made up 57% of California's population, 50% of the UC's applicants were white. In 2013, only 39% of California's residents were white, and, similarly, only 27% of the UC's applicants were.

Recently, more Hispanic students are applying and getting in. Though they made up 26% of California's population in 1990, they represented only 13% of UC applicants. In 2013, Hispanics made up 32% of the UC's applicants.
